How it works

Repositioning legs
are the deal of the year.

If your dates and route are flexible, you can fly the same airframe at a fraction of the on-demand charter price. Three things to know before you book.

— 01 · DATES & ROUTE LOCKED

Take it as scheduled.

Empty legs are positioning flights — the aircraft is already going there, with or without you. Departure window typically holds within an hour of the listed time. The route is the route; no diversion to a different city.

— 02 · CANCEL RISK

If the original trip falls through, so does yours.

The reason the leg exists is that an outbound charter is bringing the aircraft to that city. If that outbound cancels, the empty leg cancels too. Your payment is fully refunded and you get a credit toward a regular charter, but you'll need a backup plan.

— 03 · FIRST CALL WINS

One booking per leg.

Empty legs aren't held — they're sold the moment a confirmation comes through. The list updates every fifteen minutes; if you see one you want, call the dispatch line and we'll lock it on the spot. No soft-hold, no waitlist.
